Here's what the great jurist Oliver Wndell Holmes, Sr. had to say about puns:

A pun does not commonly justify a blow in return. But if a blow were given for such cause, and death ensued, the jury would be judges both of the facts and of the pun, and might, if the latter were of an agravated character, return a veredict of justifiable homicide.
Clearly, the dude was not a pun fan.
